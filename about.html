The 30% problem

Here's the dirty secret of workplace investigations: the actual investigating — talking to people, weighing evidence, making the hard call — that's the part we're good at. That's the part I love. But it's maybe 30% of the job. The rest is paperwork. Writing the same report structure for the 400th time. Building interview outlines from scratch when you already know the questions. Formatting a training deck at 11pm because someone needs it by morning.

The tools that exist are either absurdly expensive or laughably basic — and sometimes both. I evaluated every case management platform I could find, and they all had the same pitch: "Store your cases in our system instead of your spreadsheet." Great. I'm still writing the report myself at midnight.

So I built Violet around a simple principle: Violet drafts, you decide. You set the findings and make the calls — Violet handles the writing, the analysis, and the paperwork. The judgment stays with the professional. The busywork doesn't have to.
